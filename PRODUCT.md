# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Managers and team leads attending Yonatan's facilitated AI-adoption workshops. Context: a live 60-90 minute session in a bright training room, on laptops, sometimes projected to the room. Non-designer business audience; many are first-time AI users. The job to be done: leave the workshop with personally relevant AI use cases, a behavioral-science change strategy for their own team, and a one-page plan they will act on Monday.

## Product Purpose

The AI Playbook is a 4-phase workshop tool: a 7-question intake, AI-generated personalized use cases (phase "AI Use Cases"), a change strategy grounded in five behavioral-science rules (phase "Change Strategy"), then Review and export (the "Big Move" one-pager, Word/PDF). Success looks like a manager starring commitments through the flow and leaving with a plan their team actually runs. Starring is the core interaction of the whole product.

## Brand Personality

Clean, confident, energizing. A workshop tool, not enterprise software: friendly and alive, but disciplined. Miro-adjacent DNA: white canvas, near-black ink, black pill CTAs, ONE warm accent, generous rounded cards. Quiet on working screens; celebration is reserved for exactly one moment, the Big Move finale.

## Anti-references

- No themed metaphors or costume concepts (expedition/waypoint, poster-machine, color-seasons were all rejected).
- No green, teal, or lime-leaning yellow anywhere, ever (rejected three times: teal pastels, canary yellow #FFD02F, olive darks).
- Not the cream + serif + terracotta AI-default look (rejected twice).
- Not the old black/white/red magazine editorial (the shipped design being replaced).
- No palette sprawl: one accent hue plus at most a dedicated star gold.
- No invented page vocabulary: phases are exactly "AI Use Cases" and "Change Strategy"; the things on the boards are "categories" and "ideas"/"actions".

## Design Principles

1. Structure over skin: the redesign's value is the rail-focus-tray bones, the visible accumulation of starred commitments, and a poster-scale finale; not recoloring.
2. The tray is sacred: starred commitments stay visible at all times; nothing (chat included) may cover it.
3. One primary action per screen, in a predictable place: the gate bar (status left, reassurance center, single primary right) is the product's signature control.
4. Show the work: generation screens walk through categories/rules visibly (the spinner-with-progress is loved); save celebration for the Big Move only.
5. Word-for-word fidelity: labels, hints, and microcopy come from the product, never invented for mockups.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum; aim for 7:1 contrast on text because screens are projected in bright rooms. Visible keyboard focus. Reduced motion respected (subtle 0.3-0.5s transform/opacity only). Primary CTAs never the smallest text on screen (14px+).
